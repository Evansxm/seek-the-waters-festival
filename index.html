About the Festival

A Gathering of Water, Land and Ancestral Memory

Theme: Reviving Indigenous Wisdom through Land, Food, and Culture

SEEK THE WATERS FESTIVAL is a vibrant 3-day festival celebrating Eco-Afrofuturism through a blend of performance arts, live music, curated DJ sets, art exhibitions, film screenings, book readings, panel discussions and regenerative & cultural workshops that include indigenous instrument making, food demonstrations, sorghum beer making, earth building, weaving (mats, brooms, hats), Setswana dance tutorials and more.

The festival emphasizes environmental consciousness through water preservation and land restoration. Additionally, it offers recreational activities such as camping, rewilding hikes, cycling, indigenous games and creative upcycling workshops.

The festival aims to create a vibrant space for creativity, community, and cultural dialogues.

Festival Pillars

Activities & Experiences

Performance Arts & Live Music

Curated DJ sets, live music performances, and art exhibitions that blend contemporary sounds with indigenous rhythms. Film screenings and book readings enrich the cultural dialogue.

Regenerative & Cultural Workshops

Hands-on sessions in indigenous instrument making, food demonstrations, sorghum beer making, earth building, weaving (mats, brooms, hats), and Setswana dance tutorials. Every skill shared is a seed planted for cultural continuity.

Panel Discussions & Film Screenings

Engaging panel discussions on environmental consciousness, water preservation, land restoration, and indigenous knowledge systems. Film screenings and book readings amplify the cultural dialogue.

Food Demonstrations & Communal Meals

Soil to Fork dining experience and live food demonstrations serving traditional cuisine prepared from locally foraged and harvested ingredients. Meals cooked with love and ancestral methods.

Recreational Activities & Creative Upcycling

Camping under the stars, rewilding hikes, cycling expeditions, indigenous games, and creative upcycling workshops that connect body, spirit, and land.

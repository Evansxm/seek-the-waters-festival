About the Festival

A Gathering of Water, Land and Ancestral Memory

Seek The Waters Festival is a three-day Eco-Afrofuturism gathering held in the North West Province of South Africa. The festival brings together communities, knowledge holders, artists and activists around a central mission: environmental consciousness, water preservation, land restoration, and the celebration of indigenous knowledge systems.

At the heart of the festival lies the Living Treasures of Bophirima Research Project, a collaborative initiative dedicated to documenting and revitalising the ecological and cultural wisdom of the Bophirima region. The project is a partnership between Healer of the World Arts NPC, The Creative Mapping Project, and Tlholego Eco-village.

Through planting ceremonies, regenerative workshops, intergenerational storytelling, and communal meals, the festival creates a living blueprint for how communities can reconnect with the earth and each other.

Festival Pillars

Activities & Experiences

Indigenous Planting Ceremony

More than 100 staple food crops and medicinal trees planted using traditional methods passed down through generations. A living library of indigenous agricultural wisdom.

Regenerative Workshops

Hands-on sessions in instrument making, sorghum beer brewing, earth building, weaving, and Setswana dance tutorials. Every skill shared is a seed planted for cultural continuity.

Intergenerational Activities

Storytelling circles around the fire where elders pass down oral histories, seed saving techniques, and ecological knowledge to the youngest members of the community.

Communal Meals

Soil to Fork dining experience serving traditional cuisine prepared from locally foraged and harvested ingredients. Meals for up to 200 participants cooked with love and ancestral methods.

Recreational Activities

Camping under the stars, rewilding hikes through the Bophirima landscape, cycling expeditions, and traditional indigenous games that connect body, spirit, and land.
